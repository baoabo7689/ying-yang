The Rules
	The 2 × 2 Constraint: 
	No 2 × 2 block of cells can be entirely the same color. 
	You must have at least one of the opposite symbols in every 2 × 2 square.
	
	Orthogonal Connectivity: 
	All cells of the same symbol must be connected to each other horizontally or vertically, forming a single continuous group. 
	Neither color can be completely isolated from its own kind.
	
	The Given Clues: 
	Some cells start with predefined symbols (e.g., black or white circles), 
	which cannot be changed and serve as your starting points for deduction.
